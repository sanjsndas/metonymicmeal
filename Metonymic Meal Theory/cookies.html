Understanding Cookies

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit websites. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Much like ingredients in a recipe that work together to create a complete dish, cookies help create a seamless and personalized browsing experience by remembering your preferences and actions.

How We Use Cookies

At Metonymic Meal Theory, we use cookies for several purposes:

  • To ensure our website functions properly
  • To remember your preferences and settings
  • To understand how visitors interact with our content
  • To improve the performance and user experience of our site
  • To provide relevant content based on your interests
  • To support our academic research on culinary theory engagement

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

Browser Controls

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block cookies from specific websites or all websites
  • Set preferences for different types of cookies

However, please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website and your ability to access certain features.
